Brief

U4RAD Technologies is a radiology tech company. Their goal is to build XRAi Digital, a venture focused on setting up radiology clinics and at-home diagnostic services. The brief is to improve patient experience by creating an inclusive and efficient care model.

Intervention & Impact

Redesigned clinic layouts for better accessibility, developed modular imaging equipment to ease patient positioning, and created a digital triage system to streamline workflows. These interventions improved patient safety and dignity, especially for mobility-impaired users, while reducing procedure times, enhancing emotional well-being, and empowering medical staff with more efficient, ergonomic tools and protocols.

My Role

Collaborated with a cross-disciplinary team to conduct research, define patient and staff needs, co-create solutions with stakeholders, and prototype inclusive medical equipment, clinic layouts, and digital systems. Led workshops with healthcare professionals to refine designs and ensure practical implementation.

Methods & Skills Leveraged

User Research | Usability Testing | Space Planning | Ergonomic Modeling | Medical Equipment Design | Workshop Facilitation | Journey Mapping

Discovery

Researched patient and staff experiences to identify barriers in accessibility, workflow, and emotional care in radiology clinics.

Conducted interviews with patients, attendants, radiographers, and radiologists across multiple clinics, complemented by field observations and equipment analysis. This helped to understand the physical, operational, and psychological barriers patients face, particularly those with mobility impairments, and informed design strategies that enhance comfort, safety, and efficiency.

Problem Definition

Most radiology clinics in India lack accessible spaces and equipment for mobility-impaired patients, rely on inefficient manual workflows, and fail to provide adequate emotional support, leading to safety risks, delays in care, and increased anxiety for patients, while underutilized technology limits clinical efficiency and effectiveness.

Opportunity

Area of Opportunity

How might we redesign the radiology experience to be safer, more inclusive, and empathetic while improving efficiency and accuracy in diagnosis and care delivery, particularly for patients with mobility challenges, trauma histories, and special needs?

Theory of Change

By creating a connected ecosystem where patient needs are prioritized through accessible equipment, efficient triage, ergonomic design, and empathetic spatial environments, clinics can reduce procedural delays, enhance comfort, and improve health outcomes, especially for underserved patient groups.

Prototyping & Workshops

Tested and refined solutions through prototype development, usability testing, and stakeholder workshops.

Created 3D prototypes of modular equipment and clinic layouts, and developed wireframes for the digital triage interface. These were validated through usability tests with staff and patients, and refined using feedback. Co-creation workshops with radiographers and healthcare professionals helped align designs with clinical needs and ensure practical implementation in real-world settings.

Design Intervention & Outcomes

Created spatial layouts, medical equipment solutions, and digital platforms to address key accessibility and efficiency challenges.

The design interventions included: rethinking clinic layouts for smooth navigation and privacy, developing adjustable imaging equipment for safe positioning, and implementing a triage platform to prioritize patient care based on individual needs. Additionally, communication tools and peer-support protocols were designed to build trust, reduce anxiety, and foster a more supportive care environment.

Impact

Enhanced patient dignity, safety, and well-being while empowering healthcare staff with better tools and processes.

The implemented interventions resulted in safer, more efficient workflows, particularly benefiting patients with mobility impairments. Emotional comfort and trust improved through better communication and peer-support structures. Clinic efficiency increased through streamlined triage and resource allocation, while staff experienced reduced fatigue and improved job satisfaction. The project laid the groundwork for replicable, scalable models of patient-centered care in radiology clinics.
